独自上山的Mr. Sesemann看到了恢复健康的Clara，感到高兴又激动，他感谢了爷爷。Peter因为心虚和害怕，最终承认了自己弄坏轮椅的事实。奶奶并不打算惩罚Peter……


Being again urged( 被催促 ) to say a wish, he saw at last that he was saved from the policeman.

He felt as if the most terrible fear had left him.

He knew now that it was better to confess( 承认 ) at once, when something had gone wrong, so he said,

“I have also lost the paper.”

Thinking for a while, the grandmama understood and said:

“That is right. Always confess what is wrong, then it can be settled( 被解决 ).

And now, what would you like to have?”

So Peter could choose everything in the world he wished. His brain got dizzy( 晕眩的 ).

He saw before him all the wonderful things in the fair in Mayenfeld.

He had often stood there for hours, looking at the pretty red whistles( 口哨 ) and the little knives;

unfortunately( 不幸地 ) Peter had never possessed( 拥有 ) enough money to buy half of those objects.

He stood thinking, not able to decide, when a bright thought struck him.

“Ten cents,” said Peter with decision.

“That certainly is not too much,” the old lady said with a smile.

She took a bank note out of her pocket, on top of which she laid twenty cents.

“Now I’ll explain this to you. You will have ten cents every week of the year.

You’ll be able to spend them every Sunday through the year.”

“All my life?” Peter asked quite innocently( 天真地 ).

Laughingly she said,

“You shall have it, my boy.

I will put it in my will and then you will do the same, my son. Listen!

Peter the goatherd( 牧羊人 ) shall have a ten-cent piece weekly as long as he lives.”

Peter, looking at his gift, said seriously,

“God be thanked!” Jumping up, he ran away.

His heart was so light that he felt he could fly.

A little later the whole party sat round the table, holding a dinner to celebrate.

After dinner, Clara, who was lively as never before, said to her father:

“Oh, Papa, if you only knew all the things grandfather did for me.

It would take many days to tell you; I shall never forget them all my life.

Oh, if we could please him only half as much as what he did for me.”

“It is my greatest wish, too, dear child,” said her father;

“I have been trying to think of something all the time.

We have to show our gratitude( 感激 ) in some way.”

Accordingly, Mr. Sesemann walked over to the old man, and began:

“My dear friend, may I say one word to you?

I am sure you believe me when I tell you that I have not known any real joy( 欢乐 ) for years.

My wealth was nothing to me when I could not help my child and make her happy!

You have given her a new life. Please tell me how to thank you.

I know I shall never be able to repay( 报答 ) you, but I shall do everything in my power to try it.

Can I do anything for you? Please let me know.”

The uncle had listened quietly and had looked at the happy father.

“Mr. Sesemann, you can be sure that I am also repaid by the great joy I experience at seeing Clara get better,”

said the uncle firmly.

“I thank you for your kind offer, Mr. Sesemann.

As long as I live, I have enough for me and the child.

But I have one wish.

If this could be fulfilled( 实现 ), my life would be free of care.”

“Speak, my dear friend,” said Clara’s father.

“I am old,” continued the uncle,

“and shall not live many years. When I die I cannot leave Heidi anything.

If you promise me that Heidi will never have to go into the world and earn( 赚 ) her bread,

you would repay me for what I was able to do for you and Clara.”

“My dear friend, there is no question of that,” began Mr. Sesemann;

“the child belongs to( 属于 ) us! I promise at once that we shall look after her.

We all know that she is not suited( 适合 ) to live a life away from home.

Still, she has made some true friends, and one of them will be here very shortly.

The doctor is just now completing his last business in Frankfurt.

He plans to take your advice and live here.

He has never felt so happy as with you and Heidi.

The child will have two protectors near her,

and I hope with God’s will, that they may live a long, long time.”

The grandmama now put her arms around the child and said,

“Heidi, I want to know if you also have a wish?”

“Yes indeed, I have,” said Heidi, pleased.

“Tell me what it is, child!”

“I should like to have my bed from Frankfurt with the three high pillows( 枕头 ) and the thick, warm cover.

Then grandmother will be able to keep warm and won’t have to wear her scarf in bed.”

“Oh dear, I had nearly forgotten what I meant to do.

I am so glad you have reminded( 提醒 ) me, Heidi.

I shall ask immediately for the bed, and if Miss Rottenmeier sends it off at once, it can be here in two days.

I hope the poor blind grandmother will sleep better when it comes.”
