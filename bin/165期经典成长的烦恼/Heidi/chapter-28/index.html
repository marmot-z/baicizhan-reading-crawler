祖母对Peter读的诗并不满意，因为他总是漏掉其中难读的词语。天气渐渐暖和了起来，Heidi收到Clara要来拜访的来信，非常开心，不久Clara和奶奶真的来了……


Heidi pushed with all her might, and look!

The wheelchair rolled( 滚 ) easily over the dry grass.

When they passed the empty goat shed( 棚 ), Clara said pitifully,

“Oh grandmama, if I could only wait up here for Schwänli and Bärli!

I am afraid I shan’t see Peter and his goats, if we have to go away so soon again.”

“Dear child, enjoy this moment,” said the grandmama, who had followed.

“Oh, what wonderful flowers!” said Clara again.

“There are whole bushes of( 一丛丛 ) excellent, red blossoms( 花 ).

Oh, if I could only pick some of those beautiful flowers!”

Heidi, immediately gathering( 采集 ) many of them, put them on Clara’s lap( 大腿 ).

“Clara, this is really nothing in comparison with the many flowers in the pasture( 牧场 ).

You must come up once and see them.”

“Oh, grandmama, do you think I can ever go up there?” Clara asked with a wild longing( 渴望 ) in her eyes.

“If I could only walk with you, Heidi, and climb round everywhere!”

“I’ll push you!” Heidi said for comfort.

To show how easy it was, she pushed the chair.

It moved so fast that it almost fell down the mountain, but the grandfather was there to stop it at the last moment.

It was time for lunch now.

The table was set up outside, and soon everybody sat down.

The grandmama was overcome( 被深深折服 ) by the view and the delicious wind that fanned her cheek( 脸颊 ).

She remarked( 说 ):

“What a wonderful place this is!

I have never seen anything like it! But what do I see?” she continued.

“I think you are eating your second piece of cheese( 奶酪 ), Clara?”

“Oh grandmama, it tastes better than all the things we get in Ragatz,”

replied the child, eagerly eating the tasty dish.

“That’s right, eat as much as you can!” said the old man satisfied.

“It’s the mountain air that makes up for the faults( 瑕疵 ) of the cooking.”

During the meal the uncle and the grandmama had soon got into a lively conversation.

They seemed to agree on many things, and understood each other like old friends.

A little later the grandmama looked over to the west.

“We must soon start, Clara, for the sun is already low; our guides will be here shortly.”

Clara’s face had become sad.

She asked whether she could still see the hut, and the grandmama agreed.

The wheelchair was too big for the door, so the uncle quietly lifted Clara in his strong arms and carried her in.

When the uncle showed her Heidi’s bedroom, she said,

“What a lovely place to sleep! Oh, Heidi, you can look right up to the sky from your bed.

You can hear the fir( 冷杉 ) trees roar( 咆哮 ) here, can’t you?

Oh, I never saw a more delightful( 使人愉快的 ) bedroom!”

The uncle, looking at the old lady, said now,

“I have an idea. I believe it would give Clara new strength to stay up here with us a little while.

You have brought so many covers that we can easily make a soft bed for Clara here.

My dear lady, you can easily leave the care to me.

I’ll undertake( 承担 ) it gladly.”

The children screamed( 大叫 ) for joy( 欢乐 ), and grandmama’s face was beaming( 眉开眼笑的 ).

“What a fine man you are!” she burst out( 说出口 ).

“I was just thinking myself that a stay here would strengthen the child, but then I thought of the care and trouble for you.

And now you have offered to do it, as if it was nothing at all.

How can I thank you enough, uncle?”

After shaking hands many times, the two prepared Clara’s bed.

Thanks to the old lady’s preparations, the bed was soon so soft that the hay( 干草 ) could not be felt at all.

The uncle had carried his new patient back to her wheelchair, and there they found her sitting, with Heidi beside her.

They were eagerly talking of their plans for the coming weeks.

When they were told that Clara might stay for a month or so, their faces beamed more than ever.

When the grandmama got ready to leave, she said that she would go back to Ragatz, but she promised to come back from time to time.

Peter came racing down to the hut with all his goats.

Seeing Heidi, they ran up to her quickly, and so Clara met Schwänli and Bärli and all the others.

Peter, however, kept away, only sending angry looks at the two girls.

When they said good night, he only ran away, beating the air with his stick.

The end of the joyous day had come.

The two children were both lying in their beds.

As soon as Heidi lay down, she fell asleep.

But Clara could not sleep quite yet, it was too wonderful to see the stars from her bed.

In truth, she had never seen them before.

In Frankfurt all the blinds( 窗帘 ) were always down long before the stars came out, and at night she had never been outside the house.

She could hardly keep her eyes shut.

She had to open them again and again to watch the stars, till her eyes closed at last and she saw two big twinkling( 闪闪发光的 ) stars in her dream.
