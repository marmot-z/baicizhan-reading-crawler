Peter邀请Heidi跟他一起去山间，Heidi拒绝了好几次，为了等Clara的到来，但是来拜访的却是提议送Heidi回家的医生，他向Heidi解释说等天气暖和了，Clara就会过来，Heidi虽然吃惊，却热情地接待了他，爷爷建议医生住在村里，这样方便来往。


The sun was in its highest point and the wind had almost stopped.

Only a soft wind blew on the Alp.

The uncle now got up and went into the hut.

He returned soon with milk, cheese( 奶酪 ), and meat that had been dried in the pure( 纯净的 ) air.

The doctor enjoyed his dinner better than any he had ever tasted.

“Yes, we must send Clara up here. She could get so strong!” he said.

“If she would have an appetite( 胃口 ) like mine today, she couldn’t help getting nice and fat.”

At this moment, a man could be seen walking up with a large sack( 麻袋 ) on his shoulders.

Arriving on top, he threw down his load( 负重 ), breathing( 呼吸 ) in the pure fresh air.

Opening the cover, the doctor said:

“This has come for you from Frankfurt( 法兰克福，地名 ), Heidi. Come and look what is in it.”

Heidi shyly watched the pile( 堆 ).

Only when the gentleman opened the box with the cakes for the grandmother she said joyfully( 高兴地 ),

“Oh, now grandmother can eat this lovely cake.”

She was taking the box and the beautiful scarf on her arm.

She was going to race down to deliver( 分发 ) the gifts, but the men persuaded( 说服 ) her to stay and unpack( 打开 ) the rest of the gifts.

She was very happy when she found all the other things.

When it was near sunset( 日落 ), the doctor rose to start on his way down.

The grandfather carried the box, the scarf and the sausage( 香肠 ).

Together they wandered( 走 ) down the mountain side.

When they reached Peter’s hut, Heidi was told to go inside and wait for her grandfather there.

At parting she asked,

“Would you like to come with me up to the pasture( 牧场 ) tomorrow, doctor?”

“With pleasure. Goodbye, Heidi,” was the reply.

The grandfather had placed all the presents in front of the door, and it took Heidi long to carry in the huge box and the sausage.

She put the scarf on the grandmother’s knee.

Brigida had silently watched what happened,

and could not open her eyes wide enough when she saw the enormous( 巨大的 ) sausage.

She had never seen such thing in her life, and now it was hers and she could cut it herself.

At this moment Peter came in.

“The uncle is coming up behind me, and Heidi must—” that was as far as he got, for his eyes had fastened on( 集中注意力于... ) the sausage.

It was too late, and the grandfather called through the open door:

“Heidi, come, you must get your sleep.”

Saying good night to all of them, he took Heidi by the hand,

and under the shining stars they wandered home to their peaceful cottage.

Early the next morning the doctor climbed up the mountain in company with Peter and his goats.

The friendly gentleman made several attempts to start a conversation with the boy, but the boy hardly answered.

When they arrived on top, they found Heidi already waiting, fresh as the early dawn( 黎明 ).

“Are you coming?” asked Peter as usual.

“Of course I shall, if the doctor comes with us,” replied the child.

The grandfather, coming out of the hut, greeted( 向...问候 ) the newcomer with great respect.

Then he went up to Peter, and hung the sack( 麻袋 ) over Peter’s shoulder, which seemed heavier than usual that day.

When they had started on their way, the goats were all around Heidi, but she kept urging( 催促 ) them forward.

When at last she was walking peacefully by the doctor’s side, she began to tell him many things about the goats.

She told him about their strange acts, and about the flowers, rocks and birds they saw.

When they arrived at their destination( 目的地 ), time seemed to have flown.

All the time Peter was sending many angry glances( 瞥 ) at the doctor, who never even noticed it.

Now that Heidi had come again to the pasture( 牧场 ) with him, she did nothing but talk to the old gentleman.

It made him very cross( 生气的 ) that he was not even able to get near her.

When the sun was in its highest point, Peter knew that it was noon.

He called over to the other two with all his might:

“Time to eat.”

When Heidi was getting up to fetch( 拿 ) their lunch, the doctor just asked for a glass of milk, which was all he wanted.

The child also decided to only drink the milk, running over to Peter and informing him of their decision.

When the boy found that everything in the bag was his, he hurried with his task as never in his life before.

But he felt guilty( 感到内疚的 ) about his former anger at the kind gentleman.

Heidi and the doctor had wandered about the pasture till the gentleman had found it was time to go back to the village.

When they parted, Heidi waved( 招手 ) to him.

From time to time he turned about.

Seeing the child still standing there, he had to think of his own little daughter,

who used to wave to him like that when he went away from home.
