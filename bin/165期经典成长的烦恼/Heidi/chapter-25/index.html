The weather was warm and sunny that month.

Every morning the doctor came up to the Alp, spending his day very often with the old man.

The uncle would show his guest all the herbs( 药草 ) that grew on hidden places.

He could tell many strange things of the beasts( 野兽 ) that lived in holes in rock or earth, or in the high tops of trees.

But he spent most of his days with Heidi.

Then the two would sit together on the child’s favorite spot( 地方 ), and Peter sat behind them by himself.

Heidi would sing him songs, and entertain( 使快乐 ) him with all the things she knew.

At last the beautiful month of September was over.

One morning the doctor came up with a sadder face than usual.

The time had come for him to go back to Frankfurt, and the uncle’s sadness at that news was great.

Heidi herself could hardly realize that her loving friend, whom she had been seeing every day, was really leaving.

The doctor himself did not want to go, for the Alp had become a home to him.

But it was necessary for him to go.

Shaking hands with the grandfather, he said good bye.

Heidi went along with him a little way.

Hand in hand they wandered down( 往下走 ), till the doctor stood still.

Then touching Heidi’s curly( 卷卷的 ) hair, he said,

“Now I must go, Heidi! I wish I could take you along with me to Frankfurt.”

Shaking hands, they parted.

Heidi stayed on the same spot, waving( 摇 ) her hand and looking after her friend till he seemed no bigger than a little dot( 点 ).

Then he looked back a last time at Heidi and the sunny Alp, saying to himself,

“It is beautiful up there.

Body and soul( 灵魂 ) get strengthened in that place and life seems worth living again.”

The snow lay deep around the Alm-hut.

It came all the way up to the windows, and the door had entirely disappeared. Around Peter’s hut it was the same.

When the boy went out to dig a way through the snow, he had to creep( 爬 ) through the window;

then he would sink( 沉 ) deep into the soft snow and kick with arms and legs to get free.

The uncle had moved down to the village with Heidi and his goats.

Near the church lay an old ruin( 废墟 ) that once had been a spacious building.

Years ago, when the uncle had come to the village with Tobias, he had lived there.

On approaching the house from the back, there was an open room, where nearly all the walls lay in ruins.

On one side, the remains of a church room could be seen, now covered with plants.

A large hall came next, with a beautiful stone floor and grass growing in the cracks( 缝隙 ) in the floor.

The uncle had made a wooden partition( 隔板 ) here for the goats, and covered the floor with straw( 稻草 ).

Several passages led finally to a room with a heavy iron door.

This room was still in good condition.

In one corner was a huge stove( 炉子 ), which nearly reached up to the ceiling( 天花板 ).

Heidi discovered a bed placed between the wall and the stove.

“Oh grandfather, I have found my bedroom,” shouted the little girl.

“Oh, how fine it is!”

Heidi was delighted( 高兴的 ) with her new home.

When Peter arrived the next day, she did not rest till he had seen every corner of the curious( 稀奇古怪的 ) place.

Heidi slept very well in her corner, but it took her many days to get used to it.

When she woke up in the morning and could not hear the fir( 冷杉 ) trees roar( 咆哮 ), she would wonder where she was.

But as soon as she heard her grandfather’s voice outside, she remembered everything and would jump merrily( 欢乐地 ) out of bed.

After four days had gone by, Heidi said to her grandfather,

“I must go to grandmother now, for she has been alone for so many days.”

But the grandfather shook his head and said,

“You can’t go yet, child.

The snow is very deep up there and is still falling.

Peter can hardly get through.

A little girl like you would be snowed up and lost in no time.

Wait a while till it freezes( 结冰 ) and then you can walk on top of it!”

Heidi was very sorry, but she was so busy now that the days flew by.

Every morning and afternoon she went to school, eagerly( 急切地 ) learning whatever she was taught.

She hardly ever saw Peter there, for he did not come very often.

But when Heidi came home towards evening, Peter generally paid her a visit.

After a few days the sun came out for a short time at noon, and the next morning the whole Alp shone like crystal( 水晶 ).

Peter came down to visit Heidi. Afterwards( 之后 ) Heidi climbed up with him, because she wanted to see grandmother.

When they went into the room, Peter’s mother was alone at the table mending( 修补 ).

The grandmother was nowhere to be seen.

Brigida told Heidi that the grandmother needed to stay in bed on those cold days, as she did not feel very strong.

That was something new for Heidi.

Quickly running to the old woman’s room, she found her lying in a narrow( 狭小的 ) bed, covered in her gray scarf and thin blanket( 毯子 ).

“Thank Heaven( 上帝 )!”

the grandmother shouted out when she heard Heidi’s step.

All autumn and winter long she was secretly afraid that Heidi would be taken away.

Peter had told her so much about the strange gentleman that had appeared,

and she was afraid Heidi may be leaving again soon.
