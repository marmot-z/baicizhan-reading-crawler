Judy近日发现，自己其实是个很漂亮的女孩——尽管在舞会上Jimmie只和她跳了三支舞。Daddy-Long-Legs给Judy送来了生日礼物，两人关系有所缓和。最后，Judy发现，获得快乐的秘密是活在当下，而并非执迷于过去，或是空想未来。


I’m going to enjoy every second, and I’m going to know I’m enjoying it while I’m enjoying it.

Most people don’t live; they just race.

They are trying to reach some distant goal( 目标 ).

And they get so breathless( 气喘吁吁的 ) that they lose all sight of the beautiful country they are passing through.

Then before they know it, they are old and worn out, and it doesn’t make any difference whether they’ve reached the goal or not.

I’ve decided to sit down by the way and have a lot of little happy moments, even if I never become a Great Author.

You see what a little philosopher( 哲学家 ) I am becoming!

Yours ever,

Judy

March 5th

Dear Daddy-Long-Legs,

President( 校长 ) Cuyler made a speech this evening about how the modern generation is too disrespectful( 无礼的 ).

I came away from chapel( 小教堂 ) very thoughtfully.

Ought I to treat you with more dignity( 尊严 ) and seriousness, Daddy?

Yes, I’m sure I ought. But I’m afraid I probably won’t.

Yours truly,

Jerusha Abbott

April 24th

Dear Daddy,

Spring has come again!

You should see how lovely the campus( 校园 ) is.

I think you might come and look at it for yourself.

Master( 老爷 ) Jervie dropped in again last Friday—but he chose a most inconvenient( 不方便的 ) time, for Sallie and Julia and I were just running to catch a train.

And where do you think we were going?

To Princeton, to go to a dance and a ball game!

I didn’t ask you if I might go, because I had a feeling that your secretary would say no.

But it was all very proper; we had leaves of absence from college, and Mrs. McBride chaperoned( 陪伴 ) us.

We had a wonderful time—but I won’t go into details( 细节 ).

They are too many and too hard to explain.

Saturday

Up before dawn( 拂晓 )!

We walked two miles to the top of One Tree Hill to see the sunrise( 日出 ).

We had to climb up the last slope( 斜坡 ) on our hands and knees!

The sun almost beat us!

And how hungry we were when we got back!

Dear Daddy, I’m sorry, there are too many exclamation marks( 感叹号 ) on this page.

I meant to have written a lot about the new leaves on the trees and the new running track( 跑道 ), and the awful lesson we have in biology( 生物 ) tomorrow,

and the new canoes( 独木舟 ) on the lake, and my three new dresses—white and pink and blue, with a hat to match—but I am too sleepy.

I am always making this an excuse, am I not?

But a girls’ college is a busy place and we do get tired by the end of the day!

Particularly when the day begins at dawn.

Love,

Judy

May 15th

Dear Daddy-Long-Legs,

I really do love to write to you; it gives me such a respectable feeling of having some family.

Would you like me to tell you something?

You are not the only man to whom I write letters.

There are two others! I have been receiving beautiful long letters this winter from Master Jervie (with the address typed, so Julia won’t know the writing).

And every week or so a letter in very bad handwriting arrives from Princeton.

I answer them all in a quick and business-like way.

So you see, I am not so different from other girls. I get letters, too.

There goes the bell for dinner. I’ll post this as I pass the box.

Love,

J.

June 4th

Dear Daddy,

Very busy time—summer vacation starts in ten days, examinations tomorrow.

Lots of studying, lots of packing( 打包 ), and the outdoor world is so lovely that it hurts you to stay inside.

But never mind, vacation’s coming.

Julia is going abroad this summer—for the fourth time.

No doubt about it, Daddy, life is not fair.

Goodbye, Daddy.

Have a nice summer and come back in the autumn rested and ready for another year of work. (That’s what you ought to be writing to me!)

I haven’t any idea what you do in the summer, or what you do for fun.

I can’t imagine what you do.

Do you play golf( 高尔夫 ), or ride horses or just sit in the sun and relax?

Anyway, whatever it is, have a good time and don’t forget Judy.

June 10th

Dear Daddy,

This is the hardest letter I ever wrote.

But I have decided what I must do, and there isn’t going to be any turning back.

It is very sweet and generous( 慷慨的 ) of you to wish to send me to Europe this summer—for the moment I was carried away with the idea.

But my more sensible( 明智的 ) second thoughts said no.

It would be rather illogical( 不合理的 ) of me to refuse to take your money for college, and then use it instead just for amusement( 消遣 )!

You mustn’t get me used to too many luxuries( 奢侈的事 ).

One doesn’t miss what one has never had.

But it’s very hard to go without things, after one has started to think they are his or hers by natural right.

I believe that anyone can be happy no matter what their circumstances( 情况 ) are.

But living with Sallie and Julia places a great strain( 压力 ) on that philosophy( 人生哲学 ).

They have both had things from the time they were babies; they accept happiness as a matter of course.

The World, they think, owes( 亏欠 ) them everything they want.

Maybe the World does—in any case, it seems to pay up.

But as for me, it owes me nothing, and told me so clearly in the beginning.

I know these explanations seem complicated( 复杂的 ) and strange to you—but I hope you understand my meaning.

Anyway, I have a very strong feeling that the only honest thing for me to do is to teach this summer and begin to support myself.
