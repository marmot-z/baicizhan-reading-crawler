Judy与朋友们去了一趟Princeton，在那儿玩得很开心。夏天又要来到，Daddy-Long-Legs提出想送Judy去欧洲，而Judy却严辞拒绝了。Judy不认为这个世界亏欠她什么，她也不会迷恋从未得到过的东西。


MAGNOLIA,

Four days later

I’d just finished writing the words above, when—what do you think happened?

Master( 老爷 ) Jervie is going abroad( 出国 ) too this summer.

Not with Julia and her family, but entirely by himself. I told him that you had invited me to go with a lady who is chaperoning( 陪伴 ) a party of girls.

He knows about you, Daddy.

That is, he knows that my father and mother are dead, and that a kind gentleman is sending me to college.

I didn’t have the courage to tell him about the John Grier Home and all the rest.

He thinks that you are my guardian( 监护人 ) and an old family friend.

I have never told him that I didn’t know you—that would seem too strange!

Anyway, he insisted( 坚持 ) on my going to Europe.

He said that it was a necessary part of my education and that I mustn’t think of refusing.

Also, he said that he would be in Paris( 巴黎，地名 ) at the same time, and we would run away from the chaperone occasionally and have dinner together at nice, funny, foreign restaurants.

Well, Daddy, it did appeal( 有吸引力 ) to me!

I almost weakened. If he hadn’t been so bossy( 专横的 ), maybe I should have entirely weakened.

I can be led on step by step, but I won’t be forced.

He said I was a silly, foolish( 愚蠢的 ), stubborn( 固执的 ) child. (Those are a few of his insults( 辱骂 ); I have forgotten the rest.)

He said that I didn’t know what was good for me, and I ought to let older people decide.

We almost quarreled( 吵架 )—in fact, I think we did!

So you see, Daddy, I am trying to avoid temptation( 诱惑 ).

Don’t be angry with me, please, and don’t think that I do not appreciate( 感激 ) your kindness.

I do—always. The only way I can ever pay you back is by turning out a Very Useful Citizen. (Are women citizens? I don’t suppose they are.)

Anyway, a Very Useful Person.

And when you look at me you can say, “I gave that Very Useful Person to the world.”

That sounds good, doesn’t it, Daddy?

But I don’t wish to mislead you.

The feeling often comes over me that I am not at all special.

It is fun to plan a career( 事业 ), but I probably shan’t turn out a bit different from any other ordinary person.

Yours ever,

Judy

August 19th

Dear Daddy-Long-Legs,

A letter comes from Mr. Jervis Pendleton in Paris.

It’s rather a short letter.

I’m not quite forgiven( 被原谅 ) yet for refusing to follow his advice.

However, if he gets back in time, he will see me for a few days at Lock Willow( 柳树 ) before college opens.

If I am very nice and sweet and do as he says, I shall (he implies( 暗示 )) be received into favor again.

Also a letter from Sallie.

She wants me to come to their camp for two weeks in September.

Must I ask your permission( 允许 ), or may I now do as I please?

Yes, I’m sure I may—I’m a fourth-year now, you know.

I’ve worked all summer, and I deserve a little fun.

I want to see the Adirondacks; I want to see Sallie.

I want to see Sallie’s brother—he’s going to teach me to canoe( 划艇 ).

But my main reason is a nasty( 不好的 ) one.

I want Master Jervie to arrive at Lock Willow, and find me not there.

I must show him that he can’t order me around.

No one can give me orders but you, Daddy—and even you can’t always!

I’m off for the woods.

Judy

CAMP MCBRIDE,

September 6th

Dear Daddy,

Your letter didn’t come in time (I am pleased to say).

If you want me to obey( 遵守 ) your instructions, you must have your secretary send them in less than two weeks.

As you see, I am here, and have been for five days.

The woods are fine, and so is the camp, and so is the weather, and so are the McBrides, and so is the whole world.

I’m very happy!

There’s Jimmie calling for me to come canoeing.

Goodbye—sorry to have disobeyed( 违背 ) you, but why are you so determined( 坚定的 ) not to allow me to play a little?

When I’ve worked all the summer I deserve( 值得 ) two weeks.

You are being a real dog in the manger( 自己不享用又不让别人享用的人 ) about this.

However—I love you still, Daddy, even with all your faults( 错误 ).

Judy

October 3rd

Dear Daddy-Long-Legs,

Back at college and a fourth-year!

Also editor( 编辑 ) of the Monthly.

It doesn’t seem possible, does it, that so cultured a person was an orphan( 孤儿 ) in the John Grier Home just four years ago?

We progress fast in America!

What do you think of this?

A note from Master Jervie sent to Lock Willow and forwarded here.

He’s sorry, but he finds that he can’t get up there this autumn; he has accepted an invitation to go yachting( 划艇 ) with some friends.

He hopes I’ve had a nice summer and am enjoying the country.

And he knew all the time that I was with the McBrides, for Julia told him so!

You men ought to leave these kinds of tricks( 招数 ) to women; you don’t have enough experience with them.

Yours ever,

Judy

November 17th

Dear Daddy-Long-Legs,

My career as a writer is not progressing as fast as I had hoped.

I don’t know whether to tell you or not, but I would like some (silent) sympathy( 安慰 ), please.

Don’t reopen( 重新揭开 ) the wound by mentioning it in your next letter.

I’ve been writing a book, all last winter in the evenings and all the summer.

I just finished it before college started, and sent it to a publisher( 出版商 ).

He kept it two months, and I was certain he was going to take it.

But yesterday morning it came back, with a letter from the publisher.
