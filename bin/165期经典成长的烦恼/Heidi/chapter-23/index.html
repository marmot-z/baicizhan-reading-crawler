It was an early morning in the mountains and a fresh wind rocked the old fir( 冷杉 ) trees to and fro.

Heidi opened her eyes, for the sound of the wind had awakened her.

Coming down the little ladder( 阶梯 ) and finding the grandfather’s bed empty, she ran outside.

The old man was looking up at the sky to see what the weather was going to be like that day.

Rosy clouds were passing overhead( 在头顶上 ), but gradually( 渐渐地 ) the sky grew more blue and deep.

Soon a golden light passed over the heights, for the sun was rising.

After the old man had washed and milked the goats( 山羊 ), he brought them out of the shed( 棚子 ).

When Heidi saw her friends again, she touched them tenderly( 温柔地 ).

Soon Peter arrived with the other goats, with the happy Thistlefinch ahead of all the others.

Heidi was soon in the middle of them, and was pushed about among them.

Peter was anxious( 心急地 ) to say a word to the little girl, so he gave a high whistle( 哨声 ), urging( 催促 ) the goats to climb ahead.

When he was near her, he said,

“You really might come with me today!”

“No, I can’t, Peter,” said Heidi.

“They might come from Frankfurt at any time. I must be home when they come.”

“How often you have said that,” said the boy with anger in his voice.

Then he turned about and led his goats up the mountain.

Since Heidi had come home again to her grandfather, she did many things that never occurred to( 想起 ) her before.

For instance, she would make her bed every morning, and run about the hut, tidying and cleaning.

After breakfast, Heidi began the task she had given to herself.

On this day it took her longer than usual, for the weather was too glorious( 美好的 ) to stay inside.

Over and over again the bright sun would tempt( 引诱 ) the busy child outside.

The grandfather had been busy in his little workshop( 工作间 ), merely glancing( 看一眼 ) over at the child from time to time.

Suddenly he heard her call:

“Oh grandfather, come!”

He was frightened( 感到害怕的 ) and came out quickly.

He saw her running down the hill crying:

“They are coming, they are coming. Oh, the doctor is coming first.”

When Heidi at last reached her old friend, he held out his hand, which Heidi immediately seized( 抓住 ).

In the full joy( 欢乐 ) of her heart, she shouted out:

“How do you do, doctor? And I thank you a thousand times!”

“How are you, Heidi? But what are you thanking me for?” the doctor asked, with a smile.

“Because you let me come home again,” the child explained.

The gentleman’s face lit up like sunshine.

He had certainly not expected to be received in that way on the Alp.

On the contrary( 相反 )!

Not even noticing all the beauty around him, he had climbed up sadly,

for he was sure that Heidi probably would not know him anymore.

Instead, he saw Heidi’s bright eyes looking up at him in gratefulness( 感激 ) and love.

“Come now, Heidi! Take me to your grandfather, for I want to see where you live.”

He had taken her hand like a kind father, but Heidi stood still and looked down the mountain side.

“But where are Clara and grandmama?” she asked.

“Child, I must tell you something now which will make you sad, and it makes me just as sad,” replied the doctor.

“I had to come alone, for Clara has been very ill and could not travel.

Of course grandmama has not come either;

but the spring will soon be here, and when the days get long and warm, they will surely visit you.”

Heidi was perfectly amazed( 惊讶的 ).

She could not understand how all those things that she had pictured to herself so clearly would not happen after all.

It was some time before Heidi remembered that, after all, she had come down to meet the doctor.

Looking up at her friend, she was struck by his sad and cheerless face.

How changed he was since she had seen him!

She did not like to see people unhappy, especially the good, kind doctor.

He must be sad because Clara and grandmama had not come.

To make him feel better, she said,

“Oh, it won’t last long till spring comes again; then they will come for sure;

they’ll be able to stay much longer then, and that will please Clara.

Now we’ll go to grandfather.”

Hand in hand they walked together to the cottage( 乡间小屋 ).

When they reached it, she called out to her grandfather quite happily:

“They are not here yet, but it won’t be very long before they are coming!”

The grandfather warmly welcomed his guest.

They all sat down on the bench( 长凳 ) before the door, and the doctor told them the reason of his visit.

He whispered( 低声说 ) to the child that something was coming up the mountain very soon which would bring her more pleasure than his visit.

What could it be?

The uncle advised the doctor to spend the splendid( 美妙的 ) days of autumn on the Alp.

He suggested that the doctor would take a little room in the village instead of in Ragatz.

From there he could easily walk up every day to the hut, and from there the uncle could take him all around the mountains.

This plan was accepted.
