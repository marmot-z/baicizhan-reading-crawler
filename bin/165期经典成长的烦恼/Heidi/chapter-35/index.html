Peter向众人坦白了自己的所作所为，但是奶奶却奖励他每周都能拿到十分钱，爷爷希望Heidi能得到Clara一家的照顾，Heidi想要给祖母一张舒适的床，大家的愿望都得到了满足。


Heidi, in her happiness, could hardly wait to bring the old woman the good news.

Soon it was decided that everybody should visit the grandmother, who had been left alone so long.

Before starting, however, Mr. Sesemann told them his plans.

He wanted to travel through Switzerland( 瑞士，地名 ) with his mother and Clara.

He would spend the night in the village, so as to fetch( 带 ) Clara from the Alp next morning for the journey.

From there they would go first to Ragatz and then further.

Clara’s feelings( 感受 ) were divided, for she was sorry to leave the Alp, but the plan of the trip delighted( 使高兴 ) her.

When everything was settled( 被安排好 ), they all went down.

The uncle carried Clara, who could not have risked the lengthy walk.

All the way down Heidi told the old lady of her friends in the hut,

about the cold they had to bear in winter, and the little food they had.

Brigida was just hanging up Peter’s shirt to dry, when the whole company arrived.

Rushing into the house, she called to her mother:

“Now they are all going away. Uncle is going, too, carrying the lame( 跛的 ) child.”

“Oh, must it really be?” sighed( 叹息 ) the grandmother.

“Have you seen whether they took Heidi away?

Oh, if she could only give me her hand once more!

Oh, I long to hear her voice once more!”

The same moment the door was thrown open and Heidi held her tight( 紧紧地 ).

“Grandmother, just think.

My bed with the three pillows( 枕头 ) and the thick cover is coming from Frankfurt.

Grandmama has said that it will be here in two days.”

“Oh, what a good lady she must be!

I know I ought to be glad she is taking you with her, but I don’t think I shall live long when you are gone.”

“But nobody has said so,” the grandmama said kindly.

Pressing the old woman’s hand, she continued,

“It is out of the question. Heidi will stay with you and make you happy.”

“Oh, what wonderful things God is doing for me!” said the grandmother, deeply touched.

“My dear grandmother,” said Mrs. Sesemann,

“now we must say goodbye; next year we shall come to see you just as soon as we come up the Alp.

We shall never forget you!” With that, Mrs. Sesemann shook her hand.

It was some time before Mrs. Sesemann was allowed to leave,

because the grandmother thanked her over and over again.

Mr. Sesemann and his mother went on downward( 向下 ), while Clara was carried up to spend her last night in the hut.

Next morning, Clara cried hot tears( 眼泪 ) at parting from the place she loved, where such gladness had been hers.

Heidi comforted her with plans for the coming summer, which was to be even happier than this one.

Mr. Sesemann then arrived, and a few last parting words were said.

Clara, half crying, suddenly said,

“Please give my love to Peter and the goats, Heidi!

Please greet( 向...问候 ) Schwänli especially from me, for she has helped a great deal in making me well.”

It was time to go now, and Clara was able to ride proudly beside her father.

Standing on the edge of the mountain, Heidi waved( 挥动 ) her hand, with her eyes following Clara till she had disappeared.

The bed has arrived.

Grandmother now sleeps so well every night that before long she will be stronger than ever.

Grandmama has not forgotten the cold winter on the Alp and has sent a great many warm covers and scarfs( 围巾 ) to the goatherd’s hut.

Grandmother can wrap( 裹 ) herself now and will not have to sit shaking in a corner.

In the village a large building is in progress.

The doctor has arrived and is living at present in his old quarters.

He has taken the uncle’s advice and has bought the old ruins( 废墟 ) that sheltered( 庇护 ) Heidi and her grandfather before.

He is rebuilding the part with the fine apartment( 公寓 ) for himself.

The other side is being prepared for Heidi and her grandfather.

The doctor knows that his friend is an independent man and likes to have his own space.

Bärli and Schwänli, of course, are not forgotten;

they will spend the winter in a good new shed( 棚 ) that is being built for them.

The doctor and the Alm-Uncle become better friends every day.

They have agreed to share together the pleasure and responsibility( 责任 ) that Heidi brings them.

The uncle is so thankful that he cannot express it in words, when the doctor tells him that he will provide for the child.

Now her grandfather’s heart is free of care.

Even if he is called away, another father will take care of Heidi and love her instead.

At the moment when our story closes, Heidi and Peter are sitting with grandmother.

The little girl has a lot of interesting things to share, and the others are trying so hard not to miss anything.

All summer long they have hardly met, and very many wonderful things have happened.

They are all glad at being together again.
