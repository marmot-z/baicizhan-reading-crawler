和Heidi住在一起的Clara，睡得香，食欲也比之前好了许多。Clara想要去高山牧场，但是爷爷要求她尝试站起来才会答应。Peter因为Clara打乱了他原本的生活而气愤，Heidi都只跟Clara一起玩耍……


It was then that Peter noticed the wheelchair standing near the hut.

After carefully glancing( 扫视 ) about him, he rushed at the hated object and pushed it down the mountain side.

The chair fairly flew away and had soon disappeared.

Peter felt frightened( 害怕的 ) now, and he raced up the Alp,

not daring( 不敢 ) to pause( 停 ) till he had reached a place where he could hide from the uncle.

Looking down, he watched his fallen enemy going downwards( 往下走 ), downwards.

Pieces were falling from it right and left, and were blown about.

Now the stranger would have to go away and Heidi would be his again!

But Peter had forgotten that a bad act always brings a punishment.

Heidi just now came out of the hut.

The grandfather, with Clara, followed.

Heidi at first stood still.

And then, running right and left, she returned to the old man.

“The wheelchair is gone!” Heidi cried.

At this moment, a strong wind was blowing, which violently( 猛烈地 ) closed the workshop( 工场 ) door.

“Grandfather, the wind has done it,” said Heidi eagerly.

“Oh dear! If it has fallen all the way down to the village, it will be too late to go today.

It will take us a long time to fetch( 拿 ) it.”

“If it has fallen down there, we shall never get it anymore.

It will be broken to pieces,” said the old man,

looking down and measuring( 测量 ) the distance from the corner of the hut.

“I don’t see how it happened,” he remarked( 说 ).

“What a shame! Now I’ll never be able to go up to the pasture( 牧场 ),” said Clara sadly.

“I am afraid I’ll have to go home now. What a pity, what a pity!”

“You can find a way for her to stay, grandfather, can’t you?”

“We’ll go up to the pasture today, as we have planned.

Then we shall see what further happens.”

The children were delighted( 高兴的 ), and the grandfather lost no time in getting ready.

First he fetched a pile of( 一堆 ) covers, and seating Clara on a sunny spot( 地方 ) on the dry ground, he got their breakfast.

“I wonder why Peter is so late today,” he said, leading his goats outside.

Then, lifting Clara up on one strong arm, he carried the covers on the other.

“Now, march( 前进 )!” he cried.

“The goats come with us.”

They were all astonished( 震惊的 ) when, arriving on top,

they saw Peter already lying on the ground, with his peaceful goats about him.

“What did you mean by going by us like that? I’ll teach you!” called the uncle to him.

Peter was frightened, for he knew the voice.

“Nobody was awake yet,” the boy replied.

“Have you seen the chair?” asked the uncle again.

“Which?” Peter asked.

The uncle said no more.

Unfolding( 交叠 ) the covers, he put Clara down on the dry grass.

Then, when he was certain of Clara’s comfort, he got ready to go home.

The three were to stay there till his return in the evening.

The sky was a deep blue, and the snow on the peaks( 山顶 ) was shining.

The eagle( 鹰 ) was flying above the rocky mountains.

The children felt wonderfully happy.

They had been sitting quietly for a few hours, getting lost in the beauty about them.

Heidi suddenly began to long for the spot where so many flowers grew.

“Oh, Clara,” she said hesitatingly( 犹豫地 ),

“would you be angry if I went away from you a minute and left you alone?

I want to see the flowers; But wait!—”

Jumping away, she brought Clara some plants and put them on her lap( 大腿 ).

Soon after, she returned with little Snowhopper.

“So, now you don’t need to be alone,” said Heidi.

When Clara had told her that it would give her pleasure to be left alone with the goats, Heidi started on her walk.

Clara slowly handed one leaf after another to the little creature( 生物 ), who trusted her more and more.

Coming close to the child, it ate the plants out of her hand. Clara felt such a feeling of satisfaction as she had never experienced before.

She loved to sit there on the mountain side with the little goat by her.

A great desire rose in her heart that hour.

She longed to be her own master( 主人 ) and be able to help others instead of being helped by them.

Many other thoughts and ideas rushed through her mind.

The world seemed so joyous( 欢乐的 ) and wonderful all of a sudden.

Heidi in the meantime( 同时 ) had reached the spot.

As she had expected, the whole ground was covered with yellow roses.

The flowers were nodding gently in the soft wind.

But all the perfume( 香气 ) that filled the air came from the tiny little brown flowers that hid their heads between the golden flowers.

Heidi stood without moving, breathing( 呼吸 ) in the perfumed( 芳香的 ) air.

Suddenly she turned and ran back to Clara, shouting to her from far:

“Oh, you must come, Clara, it is so lovely there.

In the evening it won’t be so fine anymore.

Don’t you think I could carry you?”

“But Heidi,” Clara said,

“of course you can’t; you are much smaller than I am. Oh, I wish I could walk!”

Heidi thought for a little while.

Peter was still lying on the ground.

He had been staring at( 盯着 ) the two children for hours, unable to believe what he saw before him.

He had destroyed( 摧毁 ) the chair to get rid of( 摆脱 ) the stranger, and there she was again, sitting right beside his playmate( 玩伴 ).
