Heidi看望祖母的时候，发现祖母的床一点儿也不舒服，但是Heidi给她读书听的时候，祖母的心情又会变好，于是Heidi便想要教Peter识字、读书，这样更加方便。Peter学得很快，不久便能读给母亲和祖母听了。


Every evening now on his return home, Peter would read one song to his grandmother, but never more.

Brigida praised him, but the old woman once replied,

“I am glad he has learnt something.

But still I am longing for the spring to come.

Then Heidi can visit me, for when she reads, the songs sound so different.

I cannot always follow Peter, and the songs don’t excite me the way they do when Heidi says them!”

And no wonder!

For Peter would often leave out long and difficult words—what did three or four words matter!

So it happened sometimes that there were hardly any words left in the songs that Peter read.

May had come.

Warm sunshine was bathing( 照耀 ) the whole Alp in glorious( 灿烂的 ) light.

Having melted( 融化 ) the last snow, it had brought the first spring flowers to the surface.

A merry( 令人愉快的 ) spring wind was blowing, drying up the wet places in the shadow( 阴影 ).

High above in the blue sky the eagle( 鹰 ) flew peacefully.

Heidi and her grandfather were back on the Alp.

The child was so happy to be home again that she jumped about among the objects she loved.

Peter arrived, whistling( 吹口哨 ) and calling.

As usual, Heidi was soon surrounded( 被包围 ) by the goats( 山羊 ), who also seemed happy to be back on the Alp.

Peter, angrily pushing the goats aside, marched up to Heidi, putting a letter into her hand.

Heidi immediately recognized( 认出 ) Clara’s handwriting.

Running to her grandfather, she shouted out,

“A letter has come from Clara! Wouldn’t you like me to read it to you, grandfather?”

Peter had followed Heidi, and she immediately read aloud to her two listeners, as follows:—

Dear Heidi,

We are all packed up( 整理好行装 ) and shall travel in two or three days.

Papa is leaving, too, but not with us, for he has to go to Paris( 巴黎，地名 ) first.

The dear doctor came nearly every day this winter.

He told us all about you and the grandfather and the mountains and the flowers he saw.

He is much better since his visit, and seems younger and happier.

Oh, how I look forward to it all!

The doctor’s advice is that I shall go to Ragatz first for about six weeks.

Then I can go to live in the village, and from there I shall come to see you every fine day.

Grandmama, who is coming with me, is looking forward to the trip too.

Goodbye, dear Heidi, with much love from grandmama,

I am your true friend,

Clara.

When Peter heard this, he swung( 摆动 ) his rod( 杆 ) from right to left.

Angrily driving the goats before him, he ran down the hill.

The month of June was nearing its end and flowers were growing everywhere, filling the air with perfume( 香味 ).

One morning Heidi came running out of the hut, where she had already completed her duties.

Suddenly she screamed( 喊叫 ) so loud that the grandfather hurriedly came out to see what had happened.

“Grandfather! Come here! Look, look!”

A strange group of people were climbing up the Alp.

First marched two men, carrying a chair with a young girl in it.

Then came a stately( 神态庄重的 ) lady on horseback( 马背 ), looking eagerly right and left.

Then an empty wheelchair, carried by a young fellow, who was followed by a porter( 门房 ).

“They are coming! They are coming!” cried Heidi in her joy( 欢乐 ), and soon the party had arrived at the top.

Great was the happiness of the children at seeing each other again.

When grandmama had come down from her horse, she said hello to Heidi first, and then turned to the uncle, who had approached the group.

The two met like old friends.

They had heard so much about each other.

After the first words were exchanged( 互相交谈 ), the grandmother said,

“My dear uncle, what a wonderful house you have.

Oh, how well my Heidi is looking, just like a little rose!”

she continued, drawing the child close to her side and patting( 轻拍 ) her cheeks( 脸颊 ).

“What glory everywhere! Clara, what do you say to it all?”

Clara, looking about her excitedly, cried:

“Oh, how wonderful, how glorious( 华丽的 )!

I have never dreamt it could be as beautiful as that.

Oh grandmama, I wish I could stay here!”

The uncle had busied himself in the meantime( 同时 ) with getting Clara’s wheelchair for her.

Then, going up to the girl, he gently lifted her into her seat.

Putting some covers over her knees, he tucked( 把...卷入 ) her feet in warmly.

“My dear uncle,” said the grandmama, surprised,

“please tell me where you learned that!

I shall send all the nurses I know here immediately.”

The uncle smiled faintly( 微微地 ), while he replied,

“It comes more from care than study.”

Clara had looked at the cloudless sky above and the rocky mountain tops for a long time.

Then she said longingly to Heidi,

“I wish I could walk round the hut to the fir( 冷杉 ) trees.

If I only could see all the things you told me so much about!”
